Biography
Lata Mangeshkar was an Indian playback singer and occasional music composer. Her contribution to the Indian music industry in a career spanning eight decades gained her honorific titles such as the "Queen of Melody", "Nightingale of India", and "Voice of the Millennium".
Mangeshkar recorded songs in over thirty-six Indian languages and a few foreign languages, though primarily in Hindi and her mother tongue Marathi. She received several accolades and honors throughout her career like In 1989, the Dadasaheb Phalke Award was bestowed on her by the Government of India. In 2001, she was awarded the Bharat Ratna. In 2007, France made her an Officer of the National Order of the Legion of Honour.
She appeared in the Guinness World Records as the most recorded artist in history before being replaced by her sister, Asha Bhosle.
